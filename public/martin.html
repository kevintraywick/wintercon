All Things Martin

Visitor guide for the crew, DMs, and out-of-town players — where to eat, stay, and wander.

The town in one paragraph

Martin is a college town of about 10,000 in Weakley County, northwest Tennessee — 11 miles from the Kentucky line, roughly 2.5 hours from both Nashville and Memphis. It's home to the University of Tennessee at Martin, a walkable downtown, and the Tennessee Soybean Festival every September. Small, friendly, cheap to visit — exactly the right size for a con where everything is five minutes from everything else.

Eat & drink

Blake's Southern MillingBarbecue in a restored old mill near downtown — brisket, smoked pork, famous pimento cheese. Exposed brick and stained glass.
The Grind Mac & Cheese Burger BarBurgers + mac and cheese in every combination. Crowd-pleaser for a table of gamers.
Woodfire Pizza KitchenNew York–style pies, good for groups.
Blue Oak Oyster Bar & GrillNo-frills local seafood and steak spot.
CraveHealth-leaning menu — bowls, wraps, lighter options.
Martin's Coffee & BakeryLocal favorite for coffee and pastries (the cinnamon roll has a following). Good pre-con caffeine staging area.
Original Dairy QueenOne of the old-school originals — chili dogs and dipped cones. A genuine local landmark.

See & do in town

Downtown / Main StreetWalkable strip of boutiques, antique shops, and eateries.
UT Martin campusPretty campus walk; Vanguard Theatre stages well-regarded productions; Skyhawks athletics in season.
C.E. Weldon Public LibraryHistoric 1925 library on South Lindell — separate from our venue, worth a peek for the building alone.
Martin Recreation Complex & Festival ParkBallfields, playground, lake, walking paths, dog park.
Tennessee Soybean Festival SeptemberThe town's signature week — music, food, parade. Not con weekend, but if you're scouting Martin in the fall, time it for this.

Worth the short drive

Discovery Park of America — Union City ~20 min100,000 sq ft museum + 50-acre heritage park. Science, history, outdoor art — legitimately excellent, bring the family.
Reelfoot Lake State Park ~45 minEarthquake-formed cypress lake — bald eagles in winter (con season!), boardwalks, boat tours.
Paris, TN & the Eiffel Tower ~40 minYes, a 60-ft Eiffel Tower replica. Also gateway to Kentucky Lake and Paris Landing State Park.

Getting here & staying

Flying: Nashville (BNA) ~160 miles or Memphis (MEM) ~135 miles — both about 2.5 hours by car. Barkley Regional (PAH, Paducah KY) is closer but has limited service.

Driving: US-45E runs straight through town; Martin sits between Union City and Jackson, TN.

Lodging: A handful of chain hotels in and around Martin, more options in Union City (20 min) and Jackson (50 min). Winter rates are gentle. We'll publish a con hotel list once we lock room blocks.

Weather in January: Cold but usually mild by northern standards — highs 40s, lows 20s–30s, occasional ice. Pack layers; the Event Center is warm.
