Projected earnings — and where the surplus goes

How to read this — and how we talk about money: badges stay cheap, the books stay open. Costs come first (venue, insurance, feeding the DMs, prizes, printing), and every dollar raised and spent gets published. Whatever remains is earmarked to promote and seed the mid-summer con — that band is the real goal. The current 100–200-attendee model projects $900–$3,200 of surplus (updated for Martin Event Center venue rates): proof of concept, but not yet a funded Summer Con. Getting there takes a ~3× stronger engine — bigger sponsorships, real word-of-mouth marketing, deep community buy-in, ~20+ DMs. $10,000 stays on the chart as the stretch-stretch marker, not the mission.

How we get there — the five pillars

Player experience first

The Player Journey Map is the spine: every attendee always comfortable, always knowing what's next and what not to miss. A magical year one is the marketing plan for year two.

DMs are the engine

Every DM ≈ 5 paying seats. 300 attendees needs ~20+ DMs plus zero-DM content (tournaments, board-game library, cosplay, vendors). DM Academy turns players into next year's capacity.

Community buy-in

One Frequency Games as co-host, the Public Library as home base, UTM student orgs as campus allies, stores within 100 miles as partners, a charity angle for the town. People support what they helped build.

Word-of-mouth & virality

Shareable pieces made to travel: hype video, cosplay reels, the meme-able poster, store sign-up nights, campus champions. Every happy attendee is a broadcaster — design for it.

Money around the ticket — open books

Keep badges cheap ($20–30). The budget is carried by sponsors ($500–$1,000+ tiers), vendors, food partners, add-ons, and merch. Every dollar is published, and the surplus promotes the Summer Con.
